Frequently Asked Questions

What the purpose of National Faith and Blue Weekend?

The purpose of NFBW is to create safer, more inclusive communities by connecting law enforcement officers and the residents they protect and serve. NFBW will consist of events that will occur in communities of every kind, and will include a wide array of activities such as picnics, athletic events, forums, and community service projects. All activities will be designed to facilitate law enforcement officers and the public getting to know each other, and to building mutual understanding. We expect there will be events in every state in the program’s inaugural year, and thousands of participants.

Who can participate in NFBW?

Anyone! The NFBW website will be open in July to download an event planning toolkit, to register to host an event, and to find an event that is occuring nearby.

Are events going to require you to participate in religious activities?

At their core, all Faith and Blue events will be designed to facilitate connections between the public and law enforcement rather than endorse specific religious rituals. There will also be activities from a broad cross-section of houses of worship, ensuring that National Faith and Blue is open to the diversity of backgrounds that make up the United States.

How does my house of worship go about connecting with a law enforcement office?

The toolkit, to be released in July, will offer a script to call your local law enforcement office to indicate your interest in organizing an activity with them.

How does my law enforcement office go about connecting with a house of worship?

The toolkit will offer a script to connect with a local house of worship to indicate your interest in organizing an activity with them.

Is there funding available to organize and event?

There will be a limited number of scholarships for event costs and for boosting social media posts. Come back in August for more information on funding.

How do we go about organizing an event?

There will be a toolkit available in July that will offer step-by-step tips on how to organize one of ten different events. The toolkit will also include downloadable resources, such as flyers and programs.

When can I sign up for an event?

You will be able to sign up to host an event beginning in July. Please add your name to our email list below, and we will send out a notice when the site is open for event organizers.

How can I find a nearby event?

This website starting in July will allow you to search for an event by location and type of event. Please add your name to our email list below, and we will send out a notice when the site is open for event organizers.

Do I have to do one of the ten activities? Can I design my own?

Of course. We welcome creativity. We do ask that custom activities follow key principles, which will be published in the toolkit.

If I have other questions, how do I find answers?

You can contact us at info@movementforward.org
